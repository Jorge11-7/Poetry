II. La Parola Perfetta

Ti voglio?
No.

Ti voglio bene?
No.

Ti amo?
Quasi. Ma non del tutto.

Ti amo tanto?
Quasi. Più vicino. Ma no.

 

Quella era la parola perfetta.

Italiano

VI. Después de Mil Poemas

Arrancaré las páginas de mi cuaderno,
Subiré a donde te quise y las dejaré ir volando,

Que los pájaros se aprendan los cantos
Y le recuerden al enamorado,

Que se puede querer desde el cielo,
escribir mil poemas,

Pero el amor,

 

No es de esperarlo.

Español

Medusa

They said your stare would walk me down the stairs of hell,
And when you turned me to stone I would forever stay put.
Yet when you looked at me you taught me how to prevail,
Even though your hope was once wrongfully took.

[…]

I was already stone
before the honor of your glance.
And you who had once been wounded by a "man"
took the stone and shattered it with your hands.
